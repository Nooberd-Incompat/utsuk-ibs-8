OAuth and token-based authentication are widely used for secure authentication and authorization in various applications, including mobile devices. 

OAuth is an industry-standard authorization framework that allows users to grant third-party applications limited access to their resources on another service provider's website, without sharing their login credentials. OAuth uses a combination of HTTP requests and tokens to authenticate the user and authorize the application.

Token-based authentication involves the use of digital tokens to verify the identity of a user or device. These tokens can be obtained through various methods, such as username/password combinations, biometric data, or public key infrastructure (PKI).

In a mobile security context, OAuth and token-based authentication are often used to secure mobile applications that require access to sensitive resources, such as user data or cloud services.

For example, in the Google Play Store, developers use OAuth 2.0 to authenticate users and authorize their apps to access certain APIs and resources. Similarly, in iOS, Apple uses token-based authentication mechanisms, such as Keychain, to secure app data and protect against unauthorized access.

While these protocols are widely used in mobile security, their specifics and implementation details may vary depending on the application, platform, or service provider.
